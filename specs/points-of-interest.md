# Points of interest

Mark a location on the map that the drone should look at during flight.

## What you can do

- Place one or more points of interest (POIs) on the map.
- Set a height for each POI.
- Assign a POI to any waypoint so the drone automatically aims the camera at it.
- Get a smart gimbal pitch calculation — the app figures out the best camera angle based on the drone's position and the POI's location and height.

## How it works

1. Press **P** or click the POI button in the toolbar to enter POI mode.
2. Click on the map to place a POI.
3. Set the POI's height in the sidebar.
4. On any waypoint, change the heading mode to "Toward POI" and select which POI to aim at.
5. The app draws a line from the waypoint to the POI on the map — green if the camera pitch is correct, red if it needs adjustment.

## Good to know

- POIs are useful for inspection flights where the camera must stay locked on a building or structure.
- The smart pitch calculation uses the distance and height difference between the drone and the POI, so you don't have to do the math yourself.
- You can use multiple POIs in the same mission — different waypoints can aim at different POIs.
